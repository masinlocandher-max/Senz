# Design Rules

These rules guide future website updates before any redesign work begins.

## Main Goal

The website should be easy to read, easy to search, and respectful of the archive material.

## Keep the Current Structure for Now

Do not remove or redesign `index.html` yet. The next design work should improve the existing site carefully instead of replacing everything at once.

## Page Priorities

The most important areas are:

- Home
- Archive
- History
- Tina Sambal Dictionary
- Collections
- Timeline
- Contribute
- About

Do not add Destinations or People yet.

## Layout Rules

- Keep text readable on phones.
- Use clear headings.
- Keep archive cards organized and scannable.
- Do not hide important information inside decorative effects.
- Make search and filters easy to find.

## Content Rules

- Do not invent historical facts.
- Mark uncertain items as oral tradition, needs review, or research gap.
- Keep beginner editors in mind.
- Prefer plain labels over clever labels.

## Visual Rules

- Use the existing color palette as the starting point.
- Keep the archive feeling warm, careful, and grounded.
- Avoid making the site feel like a generic travel site.
- Avoid cluttered animation or decoration that distracts from reading.

## Accessibility Rules

- Text must have strong contrast.
- Buttons and links must be easy to see.
- Motion should not make the site hard to use.
- The site should still work if animation is reduced or unavailable.
