# Source Rules

These rules help the Masinloc Archive stay trustworthy.

## Main Rule

Do not publish a historical claim as fact unless it has a source.

## Source Labels

Use these labels consistently:

- **verified**: checked against a reliable source or reviewed by a qualified community reviewer
- **needs_review**: useful but not fully checked yet
- **oral_tradition**: remembered or shared by community members, but not yet verified by documents or multiple reviewers
- **research_gap**: a topic, date, word, or claim that still needs research before it can be used confidently

## Good Sources

Possible sources include:

- local documents
- books
- maps
- old photographs
- church or municipal records, if available and allowed
- interviews with named permission
- family records shared with permission
- community review notes

## Oral Tradition

Oral tradition is important. It should be preserved respectfully, but it should be labeled clearly.

Example wording:

> This account is currently marked as oral tradition and needs further review.

## Research Gaps

Use research gaps when the team knows a topic matters but does not yet have enough evidence.

Example wording:

> More research is needed before this date or detail can be confirmed.

## Privacy and Permission

- Do not publish phone numbers, addresses, private messages, or private family details without permission.
- For interviews, record whether the person gave permission to publish their name.
- If permission is unclear, keep the source private until reviewed.

## Editing Checklist

Before marking an item as verified, check:

- What is the source?
- Who provided it?
- When was it created or shared?
- Can another person review it?
- Is the wording careful and not exaggerated?
